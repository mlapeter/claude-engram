# Session 6 — 2025-04-01

Dana: Okay, big swirling thing. Sam got a job offer. A really good one.

Assistant: Tell me about it.

Dana: A robotics startup in Austin. Perception team lead — it's genuinely his dream problem space, and the offer is strong. He's wanted to get back into robotics for years. But it's in Austin, Texas, which is very much not Portland, Oregon.

Assistant: So the question underneath is whether you two move.

Dana: Right. And I'm torn in a way that surprises me. My job is fine — good even, and I could probably find PM work there. But my whole life fabric is here. Priya. The pottery class. The barn. The boat — I can't exactly trailer a half-restored 22-foot sailboat to Texas, and I'm not done with what that boat is doing for me. And it feels absurd to weigh a boat against Sam's dream job, but it's not really the boat, you know?

Assistant: It's not the boat. It's what the boat is tethered to.

Dana: Exactly. Mom's here. Not literally anymore — but the Gorge, Beacon Rock, the barn. This geography is load-bearing for me right now. We're not fighting about it, to be clear. Sam said flat out he won't take it if it costs us this year of me healing. But I don't want to be the reason he turns down the thing he's wanted forever, either. He has to answer them by early May, so we have a few weeks.

Assistant: A real decision with no villain in it. Those are the hard ones.

Dana: Meanwhile, normal life continues: training's at week six and going well — twelve miles Saturday, no complaints — and I made a mug with an actual handle at pottery. Small victories while the big question circles.
