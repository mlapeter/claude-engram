# Session 7 — 2025-04-14

Dana: Well. I have news and it's stupid.

Assistant: Stupid news is still news. What happened?

Dana: I sprained my ankle. Badly. Saturday, mile nine of a trail run up in Forest Park — wet root, classic story, heard something in my ankle go crunch-adjacent. Urgent care says it's a bad grade-two sprain, maybe worse; I see an orthopedist Thursday. I'm in a walking boot. And the marathon is off.

Assistant: Oh no. Off entirely — you've decided?

Dana: Decided and done. The doctor said eight to ten weeks before I can even think about running again, and you can't lose ten weeks in the middle of a marathon build and safely run 26.2 in October. I withdrew my registration this morning so I couldn't waffle about it. It's off. I'm not doing the thing where I pretend it might still happen.

Assistant: That's a clean, adult decision, and it's allowed to also feel terrible.

Dana: It feels terrible! That training arc was the first future-shaped thing I'd built since mom. Sam keeps gently pointing out that the fitness isn't lost and there's always next year, and he's right, and I don't want to hear it yet.

Assistant: Then I won't say it either. What does the next stretch look like?

Dana: Boot for a few weeks, then physical therapy. Upside: barn Sundays don't need ankles, much. I can sand planks sitting down. The Dorothy Jean might be the real winner here.

Assistant: And the Austin question — any movement while all this happened?

Dana: Still circling. Sam's deadline is early May. Honestly the ankle put it in perspective a little — more on that when we've actually talked it through. One crisis at a time.
