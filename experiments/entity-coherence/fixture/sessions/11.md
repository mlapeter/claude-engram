# Session 11 — 2025-06-08

Dana: I fixed it. Priya and I are okay.

Assistant: Tell me everything.

Dana: I finally sent the text — just "I miss you, can I buy you coffee, I want to talk about how I said what I said." She replied in four minutes. We got coffee Thursday and talked for three hours. I apologized for the way I answered — the four-second verdict — and she apologized for going polite-and-frozen instead of telling me she was hurt. The loan itself, weirdly, took ten minutes: she said on reflection she didn't actually want to owe me money either, it had just felt like the only door.

Assistant: And was there another door?

Dana: That's the good part. I spent an evening digging around and found a regional arts infrastructure grant — it funds exactly her kind of studio buildout. I offered to help her with the application; grant-shaped writing is basically my day job dialect. We spent Sunday at her kitchen table building the budget narrative. It's due end of June. Whether or not she gets it, doing that together did more repair than any amount of talking about the fight.

Assistant: You didn't change the answer, and you changed everything around it.

Dana: Exactly what I wanted and didn't know how to do. And pottery is back on — we went Tuesday and she mocked my new bowl within five minutes, which is how I knew we were really okay.

Assistant: The mockery of true friendship. And the rest?

Dana: Jogging's up to thirty minutes. Sam's first weeks at the startup are going well — his team seems genuinely good remotely. And barn news: the Dorothy Jean's hull is caulked and painted. She's close. Like, actually-in-the-water-this-summer close. I keep standing back and looking at her and getting a lump in my throat.

Assistant: Your mom's boat, nearly ready to sail again. That's not nothing.

Dana: It's very much not nothing.
