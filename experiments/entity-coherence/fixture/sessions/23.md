# Session 23 — 2025-12-20

Dana: I applied to a residency. A real one. Six months, a ceramics residency at a craft school out on the coast — studio access, a stipend, a cohort, the whole thing. Applications were due yesterday and I submitted mine at 11:52pm with a portfolio of pots I made this fall that I'm not even embarrassed by.

Assistant: Six months is a serious commitment. What would it mean if you got in?

Dana: It would mean taking a leave from work, or going even more part-time, or — I don't actually know, and I applied anyway, which tells you something. A year ago the idea of stepping back from my career for half a year to throw pots would have read to me as a breakdown. Now it reads as the sanest thing I've considered in ages. I'm not telling work until I hear back. It's a long shot — the teacher who scares me wrote my recommendation, so who knows.

Assistant: What changed, that it reads as sane now instead of a breakdown?

Dana: I think I finally understand the difference between what I do and who I am. Design — product work — is what I do. I'm good at it, it pays the mortgage, I don't hate it. But it is not who I am, and I spent fifteen years with those two things fused so tightly I couldn't see the seam. Who I am is closer to — someone who makes things with her hands and looks after the places and people her mother loved. That's not a hobby that got out of control. That's the actual center, and the job is the thing arranged around it now, not the other way around.

Assistant: "Design is what I do, not who I am." That's a clean way to say a hard-won thing.

Dana: It took me all year and a dead mother and a sprained ankle and a boat to get there, but yeah. Design is what I do. It's not who I am. I could not have told you that in February. Anyway — I'm terrified and I submitted it. Both things. Oh, and the holiday cards went out with the magenta stripe. I decided it was a feature. Sam's a good sport.

Assistant: Terrified and submitted. That's most brave things. I'll be thinking about that coast studio.

Dana: Me too. Fingers crossed and clay under the nails.
