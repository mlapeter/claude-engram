# Session 5 — 2025-03-18

Dana: I want to tell you about Friday. I don't want advice, I just want it... held somewhere.

Assistant: I'm listening. No advice.

Dana: On March 14 I finally scattered Mom's ashes at Beacon Rock. I'd been putting it off for months — the urn was in my closet and I couldn't even look at that side of the closet. But Friday was clear and cold and I just drove out to the Gorge before I could talk myself out of it.

Assistant: You did it alone?

Dana: Alone. That was right, I think. Beacon Rock was ours — she used to take me there when I was a kid, and on the way up she taught me to read cloud shapes. Not just "that one's a rabbit" — she had this whole taxonomy, what a mare's tail sky meant, when the anvil clouds meant get off the water. I stood at the top and the sky was doing exactly the kind of high thin herringbone she loved, and I let her go into the wind.

Assistant: That's a beautiful and heavy thing to have carried up there.

Dana: I picked up a fir cone from the trail on the way down. It's on the windowsill above my kitchen sink now. That's the whole story. I drove home, Sam had soup waiting, and I slept eleven hours.

Assistant: Thank you for telling me. I'll hold it exactly as you said it.

Dana: That's all I wanted. Okay. Back to ordinary things next time — I have a cutback week in training, and the taxes did get filed, for the record.

Assistant: Held. And noted on both counts.
