# Session 20 — 2025-11-08

Dana: Sam and I just got back from a weekend at a cabin near Mt Hood and I want to write it down because it was the good kind of ordinary.

Assistant: Tell me about it.

Dana: No agenda. We rented a little place with a wood stove, drove up Friday — my first Friday off, incidentally — and did almost nothing. Hiked to a frozen-edged lake, played gin rummy, he cooked, I read half a novel. And it was easy. After the two months where I kept snapping at him, I'd been a little braced, you know? Waiting to see if the awful was going to leak out again. And it just... didn't. We were us.

Assistant: So the strain really was the work-stress, not the two of you.

Dana: Completely. Off the laptop, out of the city, and I was the person he married again — well, not married, we're stubbornly unmarried, but you know. He said something Saturday by the stove that got me: "I like you at four days a week already, and it's been a week." He's noticed I'm lighter. It turns out I'm easier to live with when I'm not spending myself down to the studs at the office. Who knew.

Assistant: It sounds like the pullback is already paying a dividend you didn't budget for.

Dana: An unbudgeted dividend, exactly. The relationship was never the problem — but the relationship gets the benefit. Anyway, the whole point of telling you is: we're solid. Better than solid. I want that on the record after I spent the fall making myself sound like a person who's hard to love.

Assistant: On the record: solid. Glad you two got the frozen lake and the gin rummy.

Dana: The gin rummy was vicious and I won. That's on the record too.
