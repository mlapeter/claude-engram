# Session 19 — 2025-10-25

Dana: I did a thing that would have been unthinkable to me a year ago. I dropped to four days a week. Fridays off, twenty percent pay cut, effective November 1. HR processed it yesterday, so it's real.

Assistant: That is a big move. How did you get there?

Dana: Slowly and then all at once, like the boat. After the "mad at the laptop" week I actually asked myself what I'd do with a day back, and the answer came so fast it embarrassed me: the barn. Priya's dad's barn out in Scappoose — the one the Dorothy Jean lived in for a decade. It's falling down, honestly, and he's too old to fix it, and it's where I found my hands again this spring. So I'm restoring it. Re-siding the north wall, fixing the roofline, building a real workbench. Fridays. And I'm putting Mom's garden tools back in there where they belong instead of in my basement.

Assistant: So the Friday isn't a day off. It's a day for something.

Dana: Exactly. It's not rest, it's the barn. And a little bit it's tending Mom's stuff — her tools, and I've started keeping up the roses at the old house before it sells. Making things and tending things. I told my VP it was to "protect stabilization bandwidth," which she understood as a work optimization, and I let her believe that because the real reason is harder to say in a one-on-one.

Assistant: What's the real reason, if you can say it here?

Dana: The real reason is that the days I spend at the wheel or in that barn are the days I feel like a person, and the days I spend making roadmap tradeoffs are the days I feel like a very competent function. And I'm forty-one and I lost my mom this year and I'm no longer willing to spend five out of seven days being a function. That's the real reason. It's not burnout — the work's fine, I'm good at it. It's that it stopped being the point. Anyway. That's a lot. The gutter on the actual barn is full of ten years of leaves and I'm weirdly looking forward to it.

Assistant: "It stopped being the point" is a whole sentence. Enjoy the gutters, Dana.

Dana: I genuinely will.
