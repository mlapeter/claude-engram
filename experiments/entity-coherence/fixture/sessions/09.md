# Session 9 — 2025-05-11

Dana: The Austin question is answered. We're staying in Portland.

Assistant: That's a big resolution. How did it land?

Dana: Better than I could have hoped, honestly. Sam countered them: he'd take the role, but remote from Portland, with a week in Austin every couple months. I was bracing for them to say no — and they said yes. Apparently they wanted him badly enough. So he starts with the robotics startup in June, from the spare room, and nobody had to lose.

Assistant: So he gets the dream problem and you keep the load-bearing geography.

Dana: Exactly. When he told me they'd agreed, I cried a little, which surprised me. I hadn't let myself feel how scared I was of leaving this year — the barn, the boat, Beacon Rock, all of it. And I'd been quietly rehearsing how to be graceful about going. Not needing the rehearsal anymore was the relief.

Assistant: Did the decision change anything between you two?

Dana: Made us sturdier, I think. We did the whole thing without a single fight, which at year six feels like an achievement. He gave something up too, to be clear — the in-person team, the new city adventure. He says the trade is good. I believe him about eighty-five percent, and I'm watching the other fifteen.

Assistant: That's an honest ratio. And the rest of the ledger — ankle, Priya, boat?

Dana: Ankle: out of the boot, PT twice a week, walking normally-ish. Priya: unchanged, still frozen, still hurts; I'm not ready. Boat: starboard side is sanded and I've started re-caulking the hull seams. And Nina is two months old and I still haven't been to Denver, which is starting to itch at me.

Assistant: The ledger is mostly mending. One frozen item.

Dana: One frozen item. I know which one.
