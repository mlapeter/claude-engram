# Session 15 — 2025-08-16

Dana: So my VP pulled me aside on Thursday and basically offered to put me up for director. Formally. Head of a merged product org, the org chart doubles, the whole thing.

Assistant: That's the "bigger role" you were describing. How did it feel?

Dana: That's the strange part. Six months ago I'd have said yes before she finished the sentence — that's the rung I've been climbing toward for years, it's the obvious next move, and Elaine was practically pre-writing the promo packet. And I heard myself say "can I think about it?" And then over the weekend I told her not this year. Bad timing.

Assistant: Bad timing how?

Dana: I gave her the reasonable version, which is also true: the interoperability platform is in a fragile stabilization phase, I don't want to hand it off mid-incident, and taking a bigger org while I'm still the escalation point would be reckless. She bought it. It's a defensible "not now," not a "no." I could raise my hand again in the spring and she basically said the door's open.

Assistant: And the less-reasonable version?

Dana: I don't have one yet, that's the honest answer. It genuinely might just be the timing — this year's been a lot, Mom, the ankle, the Austin scare, and stacking a director job on top sounds exhausting rather than exciting. Which is probably grief-brain again, same as the flat launch. I'm not going to build a whole theory out of one deferred promotion. Ask me in the spring. Oh — and I fixed the faucet myself, for the record. One dollar and forty cents in washers. I have never felt more powerful.

Assistant: A director-track deferral and a defeated faucet. Big fortnight.

Dana: The faucet felt better, weirdly. Anyway — spring. We'll see.
