# Session 3 — 2025-02-24

Dana: Big announcement. I signed up for the Lakefront Marathon in October.

Assistant: A full marathon — that's a serious commitment. What prompted it?

Dana: I've been a casual runner for years, 5Ks, one half about four years ago. But this winter I needed something with a long arc, something that's just mine. Twenty-six point two felt right. I've got a training plan — four runs a week, and the long run every Saturday morning. I did eight miles this Saturday along the river and felt strong.

Assistant: How does the plan build from here?

Dana: Slowly, that's the whole trick. Add a mile to the long run most weeks, cutback week every fourth. Sam thinks I'm slightly insane but he's already talking about making a sign for the finish line. He doesn't run — he bikes — but he said he'd ride alongside me on some of the long ones.

Assistant: A support crew of one, on wheels. What worries you about it, if anything?

Dana: Injury, honestly. Every runner friend I have has a cautionary tale. I'm being careful — real shoes, rest days, not skipping the boring strength stuff. Mostly I'm just excited. It's the first time since mom that I've been excited about something in the future instead of dreading it.

Assistant: That seems worth noticing in itself.

Dana: Yeah. It is. Unrelated life-admin: I finally booked the dentist for a cleaning, first time in like two years. March sometime. Don't judge me.

Assistant: No judgment. Congratulations on the marathon — and the dentist.
