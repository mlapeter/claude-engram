# Session 21 — 2025-11-22

Dana: I said something out loud to Priya on Tuesday that I hadn't even fully said to myself, and it's been ringing in my ears since.

Assistant: What did you say?

Dana: We were at the new studio — she's got the keys now, it smells like wet clay and fresh paint, it's wonderful — and she asked, kind of offhand, whether I was going to go for the director job in the spring like I'd said. And I heard myself say, "Honestly? I'm not sure I want the director track at all anymore. I'm not sure I ever did." And then I had to sit down, because I think it's true.

Assistant: The "maybe never did" is a bigger claim than the deferral was.

Dana: It's a much bigger claim, and that's why it rattled me. Deferring the promotion, I could file under grief and timing. But "maybe I never wanted it" — that's saying the last fifteen years of climbing might have been momentum, or my mother's voice about not wasting my potential, or just what you do when you're good at something. Not actually mine. Priya, who has never once wanted a title in her life, just said "yeah, no kidding" and handed me a mug to trim, which is the most Priya response possible.

Assistant: How does it sit, now that it's out of your mouth?

Dana: Uncomfortable and correct, which is usually the combination that means something real. I'm not going to blow up my career over a Tuesday epiphany at a pottery wheel — I have a mortgage and I actually like being competent at the work. But the story where getting to director was the point of me? I don't think I believe it anymore. I think I stopped believing it and only just noticed. Anyway. Trivial: Priya's studio has an ancient printer that jams exactly like my old home one, and I found this deeply comforting. Some things are eternal.

Assistant: The eternal jammed printer. And — "uncomfortable and correct." I'll remember that one.

Dana: Yeah. Me too, I think.
